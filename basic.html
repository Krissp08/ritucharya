Discover the timeless wisdom of Ayurveda, India’s ancient system of natural healing, rooted in balance and harmony. Our platform is dedicated to exploring Ritucharya—the seasonal regimen prescribed by Ayurveda—to help you align your lifestyle with nature’s rhythms for optimal health and well-being.
Ritucharya, derived from the Sanskrit words “Ritu” (season) and “Charya” (regimen), offers practical guidance on diet, behavior, and routine according to seasonal changes. By understanding and implementing these seasonal practices, you can enhance immunity, prevent diseases, and maintain a balanced mind-body constitution throughout the year.

To maintain hormonal balance, health, and strength, Ritucharya recommends seasonal diets, habits, and routines. Ayurveda divides the year into two periods based on the sun’s position:
A. Aadaan Kaal (Uttarayan)
B. Visarga Kaal (Dakshinayan)
During Uttarayan, the sun and wind diminish the earth’s cooling energy. In Dakshinayan, lunar and moisture influences restore it. Uttarayan includes summer, spring, and late winter; Dakshinayan includes monsoon, autumn, and early winter. These cycles affect internal Doshas and influence Ayurvedic health strategies.
